Billing and Insurance Guidelines
This document outlines the standard operating procedures and patient guidelines regarding financial inquiries, insurance claims, and billing processes. It is designed to serve as a comprehensive reference for both patients and administrative staff.

---

### 1. Accepted Insurance Providers
Our facility processes claims and accepts coverage from the following primary provider, in accordance with our current network agreements:
* LIC (Life Insurance Corporation) - We accept all major health policies under LIC, including comprehensive health plans, corporate group policies, and specialized covers.
Note: Patients with other providers must utilize the Self-Pay policy outlined below and seek independent reimbursement from their respective insurers.

---

### 2. Copayment and Deductible Policies
- Copayments: Due immediately at the time of service, prior to the appointment. The exact amount is determined by the patient's specific LIC policy tier.
- Deductibles: Patients are responsible for 100% of service costs until their annual LIC deductible threshold is met. Our billing system tracks this via direct integration with the LIC portal.
- Coinsurance: Billed post-visit once the Explanation of Benefits (EOB) is received from LIC, typically representing a percentage (e.g., 20%) of the total allowed charges.

---

### 3. Self-Pay Policies
For patients without active LIC coverage or those electing to pay strictly out-of-pocket, we offer a structured Self-Pay program designed to provide transparent and fair pricing:
* A 15% discount is automatically applied to all standard procedural rates for accounts paid in full at the time of service.
* Good Faith Estimates for major procedures will be provided prior to treatment, outlining expected costs, though final charges may vary slightly based on acute medical necessity during the procedure.

---

### 4. Billing and Invoicing Process
Our standard billing cycle follows a strict resolution track to ensure timely processing for both the clinic and the patient:
1. Claim Submission: Within 48 hours of the medical service, our centralized billing department submits the coded claim directly to LIC.
2. Explanation of Benefits (EOB): Once LIC processes the claim (typically within 14-21 business days), both the medical facility and the patient receive an EOB detailing the covered portions and any contractual adjustments.
3. Patient Invoicing: Any remaining patient-responsibility balance (such as coinsurance or non-covered services) is invoiced to the patient. Payment is due within 30 days of the invoice issuance date.
4. Late Notices: A grace period of 15 days is provided, after which a 2% late fee may be applied to the outstanding balance.

---

### 5. Payment Plans and Financial Assistance
We understand that unexpected medical expenses can be a significant burden. For outstanding patient balances exceeding ₹10,000, we offer flexible, zero-interest installment plans.
* Plans can be extended up to 6 months depending on the total balance.
* To enroll, patients must contact the financial assistance office to set up an auto-draft agreement prior to their initial invoice due date.
* Hardship waivers are available on a case-by-case basis and require the submission of a financial declaration form.
