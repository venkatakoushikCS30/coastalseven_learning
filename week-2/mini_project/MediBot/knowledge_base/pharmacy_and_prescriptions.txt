Pharmacy and Prescription Guidelines

---

### 1. On-Site Pharmacy Operating Hours
* Monday through Friday: 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM
* Saturday: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM
* Sunday and Public Holidays: 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM
* 24/7 Emergency Dispensing: Available exclusively for inpatient and emergency room patients.

---

### 2. Prescription Refill and Transfer Procedures
* Refills: Prescription refills can be requested via our secure patient portal, by calling the automated pharmacy line, or in person. Please allow 24 hours for standard refill processing.
* Transfers to our Pharmacy: To transfer an existing prescription from an outside pharmacy, provide our pharmacists with the name and phone number of your current pharmacy, along with the medication details. We will handle the transfer process on your behalf.
* Transfers out: If you need to transfer a prescription to another facility, please have your new pharmacy contact us directly.

---

### 3. Generic vs. Brand-Name Drug Policies
* Generic Substitution: In accordance with standard medical practice and insurance guidelines, our pharmacy automatically dispenses FDA-approved generic equivalents for prescribed medications whenever available.
* Brand-Name Requests: If a brand-name drug is specifically required, the prescribing physician must indicate "Dispense as Written" (DAW) on the prescription. Patients requesting a brand-name drug when a generic is available may be responsible for the cost difference depending on their insurance coverage.

---

### 4. Medication Storage Guidelines
* General Storage: Store most medications in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. Avoid storing medications in bathroom cabinets due to high humidity.
* Refrigeration: Certain liquid antibiotics, insulins, and specialty injections require refrigeration (36°F to 46°F or 2°C to 8°C). Do not freeze these medications.
* Safety: Always store medications out of the reach and sight of children and pets. Keep medications in their original, labeled containers.
