Specialty Procedures and Laboratory Tests: Patient Preparation Guidelines

---

### 1. Fasting for Blood Tests
* Requirement: Most routine metabolic and lipid panels require an 8 to 12-hour fast prior to the blood draw.
* Permitted: You may drink plain water to stay hydrated. 
* Avoid: Do not consume any food, juices, coffee, tea, or chewing gum during the fasting period. Continue taking prescribed medications unless explicitly instructed otherwise by your physician.

---

### 2. Preparation for Imaging (X-rays, MRI, and CT Scans)
* X-Rays: Generally require no special preparation. You will be asked to remove jewelry, eyeglasses, and any metal objects that might interfere with the imaging.
* MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging): You must complete a comprehensive metal screening questionnaire. Inform your technologist immediately if you have a pacemaker, cochlear implants, artificial joints, or severe claustrophobia. Avoid wearing makeup, as some products contain metallic particles.
* CT Scans (Computed Tomography): If your scan requires a contrast dye, you may be asked to fast for 4 to 6 hours prior to the procedure. Inform the staff of any known allergies to iodine or shellfish, or if you have a history of kidney disease.

---

### 3. Ultrasound Preparation
* Abdominal Ultrasound: Fasting for at least 8 hours prior to the procedure is required to reduce intestinal gas and provide a clear image of the organs.
* Pelvic Ultrasound: Requires a full bladder. Drink approximately 32 ounces of water finishing 1 hour before your appointment time and do not empty your bladder until after the scan.

---

### 4. Endoscopy and Colonoscopy
* Dietary Restrictions: A strict clear liquid diet is typically required for 24 hours prior to the procedure. You must fast completely (no food or liquids) from midnight the night before.
* Medication: Specific instructions will be provided regarding blood thinners and diabetes medications.
* Transportation: Due to the administration of sedatives, you must arrange for a responsible adult to drive you home after the procedure. You will not be permitted to use a taxi or rideshare service unescorted.

---

### 5. Lab Report Turnaround Times
* Routine Blood and Urine Tests: Typically available within 24 to 48 hours.
* Imaging Results (X-ray, MRI, CT, Ultrasound): Interpreted by a radiologist and available to your primary physician within 48 to 72 hours.
* Biopsies and Pathology: Standard turnaround time is 5 to 7 business days, though complex tissue analyses may take longer.
